How to Make Spag Bolognese My Way

That is a picture of spaghetti bolognese right there, mouth-watering.


Description: I like to make this dish every now and then whenever I crave some minced beef immersed in tomato sauce and a bunch of spices, I make a pot of it that ends up lasting for a few days.

It doesn't get old in that short of a span though so unless you're not a big fan of fatty or meaty foods you'll probably feel the same way about this recipe.


So here's what you'll need:

  • 4 packs of minced beef from the store, enough to fit in a medium-large pot.
  • Cooking oil of some sort (I use peanut oil because it's what I've got).
  • Salt
  • Sugar
  • Fresh ingredients like onions if you know your way around fresh produce.
  • Store-bought seasonings of your preference (I use garlic powder, black pepper, and "italian seasoning").
  • Store-bought tomato sauce (I use meat-flavored Prego sauce for the extra-meaty taste).
  • Something to stir and break up the frozen meat with.
  • Taste-testing tool like a spoon.
  • Noodles of your preference, they don't have to be spaghetti to go well with the sauce.
  • Strainer to separate noodles from the water.

If you're ready to cook, here's how you do it:

  1. Coat the bottom of your pot with a bit of cooking oil.
  2. Put the meat inside and let it heat up until you can break it apart.
  3. Add in your fresh produce if you wish to enhance the taste.
  4. Cook the meat until most of the water has boiled off but watch out for the moment when the oils come out, you want those in the meat.
  5. Dump the tomato sauce into the pot and stir until it begins bubbling.
  6. At this point you can start adding the spices you desire, taste-test to tune the sauce to your liking.
  7. Once you've tuned the sauce, it's time to boil some water in a separate pot for your noodles.
  8. Fill the pot a bit so that the noodles will be immersed in the water and cook evenly but not displace too much water once they're all in.
  9. Wait for the water to boil before adding the noodles, stir them regularly and taste a noodle to gauge their consistency to avoid finishing too early or too late.
  10. Put your strainer in a sink and dump the pot of noodles into it to separate noodles from water.
  11. Get a plate and silverware and enjoy your delicious meal.
